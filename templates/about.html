What is the social network of intellectual collaboration?

The social network of intellectual collaboration in financial economics shows how financial economists work together. Academics frequently discuss their work with their colleagues: at conferences, when holding a visiting position at another department, or in casual discussions with other faculty members around the water cooler.

They exchange new ideas, point to emerging trends, share new literature and critize each others work, all of which can be subsumed as social informal collaboration. Typically, authors acknowledge informal collaboration in a separate footnote in a published article.

These acknowledgements form the core of the CoFE dataset (Collaboration in Financial Economics). We use the CoFE dataset to create three networks:

  • In the network of informal collaboration, authors and acknowledged commenters are connected by undirected and weighted links.

  • As comparison, pure co-author networks connect authors whenever they jointly published a paper in our dataset.

  • The network of intellectual collaboration is the compose of the two.

The networks allow us to study network effects and the dissemation of information in a large scientific setting.

We set up this website because we thought our colleagues might be interested to learn about patterns of informal collaboration and see where they are in our data. And of course because we had fun setting up the website.
